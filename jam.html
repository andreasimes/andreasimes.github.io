Jam

Paris, France Summer 2015

OVERVIEW

Jam is a French startup that has launched an SMS-based app that acts as a personal assistant for students. I interned as a UX/UI designer at the startup in summer 2015.

MY ROLE

The internship culminated in two major projects: a reconceptualization of the startup's usability testing process, which further developed my understanding of the importance of empathy in the world of user experience and a website redesign that allowed me to practice and hone my visual design skills in Sketch.

User interviews

As an SMS and Facebook Messenger-based product, perceiving how users interact with the personal assistant, including points of friction or satisfaction, was more difficult than if the product had been web or app based. Paying special attention to this, I reformulated interview questions in more neutral terms that encouraged the interviewer to put aside their stake in the product and empathize with the user. Working as a bridge between my developer teammates and the users themselves, I acted as a translator—not necessarily of different languages, but of different mindsets and perspectives.

Website redesign

After helping to develop a cohesive brand identity for Jam, I created the following pages.

The process of creating the homepage involved repositioning existing assets while the team, press, and contact pages, as well as the footer, were made from scratch. For the pages I designed from scratch, my process consisted of gathering requirements from the startup's product and executive teams, sketching out my ideas on paper, getting feedback from teams, working in Sketch to create high fidelity mockups, and then passing on the assets and CSS properties to frontend developers using Zeplin.

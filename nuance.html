Balancing sound logic with user needs

My user interface design internship at Nuance Communications

Nuance

New York, NY Summer 2016

OVERVIEW

Nuance is one of the world's largest providers of speech applications, including voice recognition software and interactive voice response (IVR) systems. During the summer of 2016, I interned as a voice user interface (VUI) designer at Nuance's New York office in the Enterprise Division.

MY ROLE

As a VUI designer, my main responsibility was to define the specifications for clients' new or redesigned IVR systems.

During the internship, I worked on two client projects. The first client, a large health insurance company had contracted with Nuance to redesign its existing IVR. I played a large role in overhauling existing portions of the system as well as defining new call flows. In a second project, I led an onsite Wizard of Oz style testing session of a regional utility company's IVR system.

Prototyping using voice, sound, and conversation

Throughout most of the internship, I helped work on the redesign of a healthcare client's IVR system. I diagrammed and prototyped new logical flows, updated prompts, and collaborated with developers to make sure my specifications were as precise and transparent as possible.

The most fulfilling part of the project was when I created prompts, grammars, and logic conditions for a complex and very visible portion of the projectâ€™s user interface, where complex information returned from the backend is organized and grouped so that it can be played in a natural, streamlined manner to callers. Specifying this flow drew heavily on my knowledge of Java and algorithms to produce an elegant design that would be easily understood by both the customer as well as the development team.

Meeting the user ear-to-ear:
Wizard of Oz usability testing

I also participated in onsite usability testing of a client's redesigned IVR system. The style was designed as Wizard of Oz-style, meaning that we would be able to analyze how users interacted with a system that was newly redesigned, but not fully programmed. During the study, I was the Wizard—while users thought they were interacting with an automated IVR system, I was "behind the curtain" reacting to their every response. I can't share more about the results of the testing here, but I'm always happy to answer questions over the phone, Skype, or over coffee!

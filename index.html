A Bay Area native, I grew up in Silicon Valley’s backyard.

FALL 2013

I moved to Reims, France to explore the social sciences at Sciences Po.

SUMMER 2015

In the summer of 2015, I discovered the world of design during a summer at Jam in Paris

FALL 2015

I moved to New York City to join the Dual BA between Sciences Po and Columbia University and to start a second bachelor's degree in Information Science.

SEPTEMBER 2015

I'm accepted into the Barnard-Columbia studio of Design for America.

SUMMER 2015

I intern at Nuance Communications within the Enterprise Division. At Nuance, I get to prototype call flows, meet with customers, and be a wizard!

SEPTEMBER 2016

I became the Director of Education within Barnard-Columbia Design for America and put my love of design to work crafting an engaging and thoughtful design curriculum.

SEPTEMBER 2016

I become the Director of Design at the Columbia European Society and undertake a redesign of their logo and brand identity.

OCTOBER 2016

I intern as a UI designer within the Communication team at Columbia's International Students and Scholars Office and gain experience transforming the confusing world of immigration regulations into more bite-size, intuitive chunks of information.

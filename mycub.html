A redesign of Columbia's job search engine

OVERVIEW

As a studio member of Barnard-Columbia Design for America, I was part of a team working on a proposed redesign of Columbia University's job portal.

MY ROLE

My role as the lead user interface designer on the team provided me with a great opportunity to further hone my technical design skills during the phase of prototype creation.

Research

The design process began with user research within the Columbia undergraduate student body.

We publicized a survey via social media and analyzed the responses both quantitatively and qualitatively in order to most accurately inform our proposed redesign of the job portal site.

Prototyping

To address concerns about both the search function as well as the job application process, we conceptualized the two cornerstones of our redesign: a new search page and a briefcase feature that would act as the hub for the application process. Inspired by e-commerce implementations of search filtering, we repositioned the search filters to the side and conceptualized a live search functionality that would update with every box ticked.
